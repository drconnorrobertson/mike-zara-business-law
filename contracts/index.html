Contract Drafting, Review & Negotiation

Every business relationship starts with a contract. Make sure yours protects you.

Schedule a Consultation (866) 823-8288

Contracts are the foundation of every business relationship. A well-drafted contract defines expectations, allocates risk, and provides a clear framework for resolving disputes. A poorly drafted one can expose your business to significant financial and legal liability.

At Zara Business Law, we draft, review, and negotiate contracts that protect your interests while maintaining the flexibility your business needs to operate and grow. Attorney Michael Zara takes a practical, business-focused approach to contract work, understanding that contracts need to be more than just legally sound. They need to be clear, enforceable, and aligned with your business objectives.

Whether you need a single agreement reviewed before signing or a complete library of contract templates for your growing business, we provide the expertise you need. We handle contracts for businesses in every industry and at every stage, from startups drafting their first customer agreements to established companies negotiating complex multi-party transactions.

Our Contract Services

We handle the full lifecycle of business contracts, from initial drafting through negotiation, execution, and enforcement. If a contract dispute arises, our commercial litigation practice is prepared to protect your interests in court or through alternative dispute resolution.

Contract Drafting

We draft clear, comprehensive contracts tailored to your specific business needs. Every agreement is built from scratch or customized from proven templates to address the unique risks and requirements of your transaction.

Contract Review & Analysis

Before you sign any agreement, our thorough review process identifies unfavorable terms, missing protections, ambiguous language, and potential liabilities that could put your business at risk.

Contract Negotiation

We represent your interests in contract negotiations, working to achieve terms that protect your business while preserving the commercial relationship. We handle direct negotiations, redlining, and multi-round revisions.

Non-Disclosure Agreements

NDAs protect your confidential information in business relationships, potential partnerships, and employment contexts. We draft mutual and one-way NDAs tailored to the sensitivity of the information involved.

Service & Vendor Agreements

Clear agreements with service providers and vendors prevent disputes and establish expectations for quality, timelines, payment terms, and liability. We draft and review agreements on both sides of these relationships.

Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation

Protect your business from competitive threats when employees or partners depart. We draft enforceable restrictive covenants that comply with the evolving legal landscape across all 50 states.

Frequently Asked Questions

Any contract that involves significant financial exposure, long-term obligations, intellectual property, employment terms, or potential liability should be reviewed by a business attorney before you sign. The cost of a contract review is almost always far less than the cost of a dispute arising from unfavorable terms you did not catch.

An enforceable contract requires offer and acceptance, consideration (something of value exchanged), mutual assent, legal capacity of the parties, and a lawful purpose. Beyond these basics, enforceability often depends on proper drafting, including clear terms, appropriate representations, and compliance with any applicable regulations.

Templates can serve as a starting point, but generic templates often fail to address the specific risks and requirements of your business and industry. Critical issues like indemnification, limitation of liability, IP ownership, and dispute resolution provisions need to be tailored to your situation. We often start with proven templates and customize them to your needs, which saves time while ensuring proper protection.

While every contract is different, key clauses typically include indemnification, limitation of liability, termination provisions, intellectual property ownership, confidentiality, dispute resolution (arbitration vs. litigation), force majeure, and governing law. Read our blog post on common contract mistakes for more details.

Contract drafting costs vary based on complexity, length, and the nature of the agreement. Simple agreements like NDAs may cost a few hundred dollars, while complex multi-party agreements can be significantly more. We provide transparent pricing and can often offer fixed-fee arrangements for standard contract work.
